A Web Developer designs, codes, and maintains websites and web applications, ensuring they are visually appealing, user-friendly, and functional. They bridge design and technical requirements by using languages like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, while managing site performance, security, and responsiveness across devices. They often collaborate with designers and stakeholders to meet project goals. 

Key Responsibilities
Development: Write clean, efficient, and testable code using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and relevant frameworks (e.g., React, Angular).
Design Implementation: Collaborate with designers to transform wireframes and mockups into functional user interfaces.
Maintenance &amp; Security: Perform regular maintenance, updates, and backups to ensure site security and performance.
Database &amp; API Integration: Integrate front-end components with back-end services, databases, and third-party APIs.
Testing &amp; Troubleshooting: Debug, test, and optimize applications for maximum speed and scalability across different browsers and devices. 

Required Skills &amp; Qualifications
Technical Proficiency: Deep knowledge of HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and backend languages (e.g., PHP, Python, Node.js).
Frameworks &amp; CMS: Experience with front-end frameworks (React, Angular, Vue) and content management systems (WordPress, Drupal).
Version Control: Proficiency in Git/GitHub for collaborative development.
Responsive Design: Expertise in creating mobile-friendly, responsive layouts.
Problem-Solving: Strong analytical skills to debug and solve complex technical issues. 


Typical Requirements
Bachelor&#39;s degree in Computer Science, IT, or related field (or equivalent experience).
2–5+ years of experience in web development.
Understanding of SEO principles and web accessibility standards.
